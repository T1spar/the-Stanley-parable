What Is The Stanley Parable?

The Stanley Parable is a 2013 first‑person “walking simulator” developed by Galactic Café. Walking simulators are narrative‑driven games that focus on exploration and story rather than combat or complex mechanics.

Basic Premise

You play as Stanley, Employee 427, who realises one day that all his co‑workers have vanished. A disembodied Narrator describes everything Stanley is “supposed” to do—but you can obey, disobey, or wander off entirely.

Choice Structure

Despite dozens of possible endings, the game reveals that every path was pre‑written. Your apparent freedom is part of the design, challenging the player to question whether choice in games (and life) is real or illusory.

Artist Statement

I chose an interactive website because it mirrors the game’s invitation to click, wander, and question agency. By sprinkling meta‑jokes (fake achievements, pop‑up Narrator), I collapse the distance between player and critic, letting the analysis perform its argument.

Confusion is intentional: it exposes how easily we follow rules even when they’re arbitrary.
